Economic:
    The most common is economic, women always have less pay than men, it’s unfair. For example female - dominated occupations such as childcare and restaurant service continues to be the lower rungs of the U.S. Women make up 63 percent of workers earning the federal minimum wage, a wage rate stuck at $7.25 since 2009. By contrast, women represent only 5 percent of CEOs at Fortune 500 firms. Also American women got the right to vote in 1920.


    The gender poverty gap widened over the past 50 years. In 1968, 10.8 percent of women in age 18-64 (6.1 million women) and 7.2 percent of men (3.7 million) in this age group lived below the poverty line. Households led by single women with children had a poverty rate of 35.6 percent, more than twice the 17.3 percent rate for households led by single men with children, according to the National Women’s Law Center.(Gender Economic Inequality)
